About this resource

The HK-ELE teacher-facing database is designed to help teachers make evidence-informed vocabulary decisions. It brings together Hong Kong English frequency evidence, school-text exposure, external grade-level reference information, academic/subject list membership, and word-part information. It is not intended to prescribe what teachers must teach; it is a planning support for professional judgement.

How teachers can use this database

1. Begin with your unit or text

Identify candidate vocabulary from a curriculum topic, textbook passage, reading text, or AI-generated text.

2. Check whether the word is worth attention

Use the HK word-family frequency band, academic/subject signals, and school exposure evidence to decide whether a word is likely to have instructional value.

3. Consider learner level

Use textbook first-seen grade and the external grade reference as reference points. These are supports for judgement, not fixed rules.

4. Plan how to teach the word

Use baseword, roots, prefixes, and suffixes to plan meaning-in-context work, morphology instruction, semantic links, and review.

Suggested interpretation: frequent word families may be useful but already known; lower-frequency words may still be important for a specific curriculum topic. Teachers should combine database evidence with knowledge of students, text demands, and lesson goals.

Column guide

Word

The word form shown in the database.

Baseword

The central word-family unit. Frequency ranking is based on Baseword, not separate word forms.

HK Word-family Frequency Band

A teacher-friendly local frequency band calculated at the Baseword / word-family level. Related word forms are grouped together to show the broader frequency and teaching value of the word family.

HK Word-family Frequency Rank

The rank of the Baseword in Hong Kong English frequency evidence. Rank 1 is the most frequent Baseword family.

Frequency Evidence

Indicates whether frequency evidence appears in both ICE-HK and GloWbE-HK or only one source.

External Grade Reference

Shows grade-level reference information from VXGL (Vocabulary eXpected Grade Level), such as Grades 6–7, based on the U.S. educational system.

Academic / Subject List

Shows academic or subject-list membership, such as AWL or MSVL.

First Seen in HK Textbooks

Shows the first grade in which the word appears in Hong Kong textbook evidence, where available.

Greek / Latin Root

Useful for teaching academic vocabulary through roots and word families.

Root Meaning

Explains the meaning of the listed Greek or Latin root, where available.

Prefix

Shows a potentially useful prefix for helping students understand form-meaning relationships.

Suffix

Shows a potentially useful suffix for helping students understand word families and derived forms.

Important notes

  • This database supports teacher judgement. It does not replace curriculum knowledge or knowledge of students.
  • HK frequency is based on Baseword / word family. This supports word-family and morphology-oriented vocabulary teaching.
  • External grade reference. VXGL (Vocabulary eXpected Grade Level) offers an additional grade-level reference based on the U.S. educational system.
  • Word parts should be taught selectively. Roots, prefixes, and suffixes are useful when they are transparent and helpful for the target lesson.
