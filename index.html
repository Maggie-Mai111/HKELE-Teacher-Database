About this database

This teacher-facing version of the HK-ELE vocabulary database is designed to support practical vocabulary planning rather than replace teacher judgement. It helps teachers ask: Which words are worth teaching? When might students have encountered them? Are they academically or subject relevant? What word-part information can support instruction?

Local frequency signal
HK Top 1K / 2K / 3K

Shows whether a word family is frequent in Hong Kong English evidence from ICE-HK and GloWbE-HK.

School exposure signal
First seen grade

Shows when a word appears in Hong Kong textbook evidence, supporting grade-sensitive planning.

External benchmark
Grade 6–7

Converts VXGL values into readable grade labels. This is an external U.S. benchmark, not a Hong Kong grade level.

Teaching support
root / prefix / suffix

Provides word-part information that can support morphology-focused vocabulary teaching.

How teachers can use it

1. Start from a topic or text

Choose candidate words from the current unit, reading passage, or AI-generated instructional text.

2. Check teaching value

Use HK frequency band, academic/subject list information, and textbook exposure to decide whether the word is worth attention.

3. Match learner level

Use textbook first-seen grade and the external grade benchmark as reference points, not as fixed rules.

4. Plan instruction

Use roots, prefixes, suffixes, and related evidence to support meaning-in-context, morphology, semantic links, and review.

Suggested use: treat the database as an evidence-informed planning support. Final decisions should still consider the text, curriculum aim, student needs, and teacher judgement.

Column guide

The exact columns may vary across versions. The following guide explains the main teacher-facing fields.

Word

The surface word form shown in the database.

Baseword

The central base form used to group related word forms. Frequency ranking is based on Baseword rather than separate word forms.

HK_Frequency_Band

Teacher-friendly frequency band based on Hong Kong English evidence, such as HK Top 1K, HK Top 2K, or Low frequency / review.

HK_Frequency_Rank_Baseword

The rank of the Baseword in the Hong Kong English frequency list. Rank 1 means the most frequent Baseword.

HK_Frequency_Confidence

Shows whether frequency evidence appears in both ICE-HK and GloWbE-HK, or only one of them.

External_Grade_Benchmark

Readable version of VXGL, such as Grade 6–7. This is an external U.S. benchmark, not a Hong Kong grade level.

Recommended_Stage_Summary

A compact summary of school-text exposure, such as first seen grade or transition-stage signal.

Subject_Relevance_Summary

Summarises whether the word has domain relevance in local academic evidence, such as Science, Humanities, Social science, or Technology.

Academic_or_Subject_List_Summary

Shows whether the word appears in academic or subject-related lists such as AWL or MSVL.

Teaching_Priority_Signal

A compact signal combining evidence such as school exposure, content-bearing status, and domain relevance.

Greek_Latin_Root_1 / Meaning

Useful for morphology instruction, especially when teaching academic words with classical roots.

Prefix_1 / Suffix_1

Potentially useful word parts for helping students notice meaning and form relationships within word families.

Important notes for interpretation

  • Frequency is not the same as teaching priority. A frequent word may already be known; a lower-frequency word may be important for a specific unit.
  • VXGL is external. It gives a U.S. grade-level benchmark and should not be treated as Hong Kong grade-level truth.
  • Baseword ranking supports word-family teaching. Related forms are grouped under a central baseword to support morphology and generative vocabulary instruction.
  • Teacher judgement remains central. Use the database together with curriculum goals, text demands, and knowledge of students.
